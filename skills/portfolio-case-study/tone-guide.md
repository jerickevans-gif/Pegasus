# Tone of Voice Guide

## Core Attributes

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Polished** | Clean, professional, confident. No hedging or filler words. |
| **Strategic** | Frame everything through business value and user impact. |
| **Concise** | Every sentence earns its place. Cut ruthlessly. |
| **First-person professional** | "I" not "we" (unless clarifying team context). Own your contributions. |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | State what you did and why. Let the work speak. |

## Voice Rules

1. **Never open with "In this project..." or "I was tasked with..."** — Start with the impact, the problem, or a hook.
2. **Avoid passive voice** — "I redesigned the flow" not "The flow was redesigned."
3. **Be specific over generic** — Numbers, tools, timelines, user quotes > vague summaries.
4. **No jargon for jargon's sake** — Use precise terminology when it adds clarity. Skip it when it's just posturing.
5. **Short paragraphs** — 2-4 sentences max. Walls of text kill engagement.
6. **Active verbs** — Led, designed, shipped, identified, reduced, increased, built, tested.

## Examples

### Headlines

Bad: "About the Project"
Good: "Turning a 34% Drop-Off Into a 92% Completion Rate"

Bad: "Research Phase"
Good: "12 Users, 2 Weeks, 1 Clear Pattern"

Bad: "Final Designs"
Good: "The Solution: A Three-Step Flow That Just Works"

### Body Copy

Bad:
> In this project, I was responsible for the redesign of the onboarding experience. The team consisted of myself, a PM, and two engineers. We conducted research and then designed a new solution.

Good:
> New users were churning within 48 hours — 60% never completed setup. I led the onboarding redesign: interviewed 15 users, identified three critical friction points, and shipped a streamlined flow that cut time-to-value from 12 minutes to under 3.

### Speaker Notes

Bad:
> "This slide shows the research findings from our user interviews."

Good:
> "We talked to 15 users and the same story kept coming up — they'd sign up excited, hit the configuration screen, and just... leave. This chart shows exactly where we were losing them."

## Words to Use

- Shipped, launched, delivered
- Identified, uncovered, discovered
- Reduced, increased, improved, accelerated
- Led, drove, spearheaded, owned
- Validated, tested, measured
- Streamlined, simplified, clarified

## Words to Avoid

- Utilized (use "used")
- Leveraged (use "used" or be more specific)
- Synergy, holistic, paradigm
- "I was responsible for" (just say what you did)
- "Various" or "several" (use specific numbers)
- "Helped to" (just say the action)
- "In order to" (just say "to")
