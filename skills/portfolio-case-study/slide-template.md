# Slide-by-Slide Output Template

Use this exact format for each slide in the outline. Adapt the number of slides (8-14) based on project depth.

---

## Slide Format

```markdown
### Slide [N]: [Section Label]

**Headline:** [Punchy, strategic headline — not a description, a statement]

**Body:**
[2-4 sentences of supporting copy. Sets context or delivers the key point. Concise.]

**Visual:** `[relative/path/to/asset.png]` — [Brief description of what the visual shows and why it's here]

**Speaker Notes:**
> [What you'd actually say when presenting this slide. Conversational tone. 3-5 sentences. Include any anecdotes, context, or emphasis points.]
```

---

## Standard Slide Sequence

Adapt this sequence based on the chosen case study structure. Not every project needs every slide.

### Opening (1-2 slides)
1. **Title Slide** — Project name, your role, one-line hook
2. **The Problem** — What was broken, what was at stake

### Context (1-2 slides)
3. **Background/Context** — Company, product, market situation
4. **The Brief** — What you were asked to do, constraints, goals

### Process (3-5 slides)
5. **Research & Discovery** — What you learned, from whom, key insights
6. **Key Insight** — The "aha moment" that shaped the direction
7. **Exploration** — Options considered, trade-offs, early concepts
8. **Design Decisions** — Why you chose this path (show reasoning, not just output)
9. **Iteration** — How it evolved based on testing/feedback

### Solution (2-3 slides)
10. **The Solution** — Final designs, key screens/deliverables
11. **How It Works** — Walkthrough of the core experience
12. **Details That Matter** — Micro-interactions, edge cases, polish

### Impact (1-2 slides)
13. **Results & Impact** — Metrics, outcomes, stakeholder response
14. **Reflection** — What you learned, what you'd do differently, what's next

## Growth Design Slide Sequence

Use this sequence when the **Growth Design** structure is chosen. Aim for 10-14 slides.

### Impact (1 slide)
1. **Impact First** — Lead with the headline metric or result. Hook the audience immediately.

### Context (1-2 slides)
2. **Business Context** — Product, team, growth stage, and why this work mattered.
3. **The Problem (with Evidence)** — Data, funnel drops, or user evidence that proves the problem was real.

### Insights & Constraints (2 slides)
4. **Key Insights** — The 2-3 non-obvious learnings that shaped the direction.
5. **Constraints** — What made this hard: timeline, tech debt, eng capacity, org dynamics.

### Strategy & Iteration (3-5 slides)
6. **The Strategy** — Your deliberate approach: hypothesis, prioritization, expected leverage.
7. **Iteration 1** — Hypothesis → test → result → learning.
8. **Iteration 2** — Next hypothesis → test → result → pivot or double-down.
9. **Iteration 3 (optional)** — Further refinement if applicable.

### Validation (2 slides)
10. **Qualitative Validation** — User feedback, session recordings, sentiment shifts.
11. **Quantitative Validation** — A/B results, funnel improvements, retention curves, statistical confidence.

### Outcomes (1-2 slides)
12. **Layered Outcomes** — Impact at multiple levels: user behavior, product metric, business outcome, team learning.
13. **What's Next / Reflection** — What this unlocked, what you'd do differently, next bets.

## Tips for Using This Template

- **Not every slide needs a unique image.** Some slides work with diagrams, charts, or annotated screenshots.
- **Headlines should be quotable.** If someone only reads headlines, they should get the story.
- **Body copy supports the visual.** Don't describe what people can see — add context they can't.
- **Speaker notes are your secret weapon.** This is where personality and storytelling live.
- **If a slide feels weak, cut it.** 10 strong slides > 14 slides with filler.
